Buying checklist

  • Match lug depth to terrain: shallow for dry, deep for mud.
  • Consider protection: rock plates help on stony paths.
  • Ultra runners need cushioning + grip for long outings.

FAQs

  • Do trail shoes work on road? Yes, short sections are fine — but deep lugs wear quickly.
  • How often replace trail shoes? Every 300–500 miles, or when lugs are visibly blunt.
  • Do I need waterproofing? Only if you spend hours in cold bogs; otherwise, draining mesh dries faster.
