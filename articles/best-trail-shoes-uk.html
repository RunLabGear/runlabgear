Best Trail Running Shoes for Muddy UK Conditions

British trails are rarely dry. In winter and shoulder seasons you need aggressive lugs, a secure midfoot and uppers that drain quickly. These models handle saturated ground without becoming planks on hardpack connectors.

  • Salomon Speedcross 6: Chevron lugs bite through clay and pasture. Precise fit, protective upper. ~£130. Buy
  • Inov‑8 X‑Talon: Low, nimble and vicious grip; a favourite for fell and cross‑country races. ~£120. Buy
  • Brooks Cascadia 17: Rock plate and stable platform for mixed terrain. ~£135. Buy
  • HOKA Speedgoat 5: Cushioned with sticky outsole; good for longer efforts on varied trails. ~£145. Buy

Key features for mud

  • 4–6 mm lugs spaced to shed mud rather than clog.
  • Midfoot lockdown to prevent lateral slippage on cambers.
  • Fast‑draining mesh; waterproof membranes only when it’s very cold.

Sizing tip: if you wear thick winter socks, consider going up half a size. Add gaiters in deep mud to reduce debris ingress.
