Buying checklist

  • Try both stability and neutral — guidance varies across brands.
  • Look for smooth transitions, not harsh correction.
  • Check width options, especially for Brooks and New Balance.

FAQs

  • Do I need stability shoes? Only if you overpronate heavily or find neutral shoes unstable.
  • Are stability shoes heavier? Often slightly, but new designs reduce the weight gap.
  • Can I race in them? Yes — many runners do marathons in models like the Kayano or Guide.
