How to Choose the Right Running Shoe for Your Foot Type

The right shoe depends on foot shape, arch height, ankle mobility and training volume. Use this checklist to narrow options and avoid common buying mistakes.

Quick Assessment

  • Length & width: Aim for a thumb’s width at the toes and a secure heel.
  • Arch & pronation: If you overpronate with recurring issues, try a mild-stability model.
  • Use case: Easy miles vs tempo vs race day — one shoe rarely
