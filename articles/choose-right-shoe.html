How to Choose the Right Running Shoe for Your Foot Type

The right shoe depends on foot shape, arch height, ankle mobility and training volume. Use the checklist below to narrow your options and avoid common buying mistakes.

Quick assessment

  • Length & width: Aim for a thumb’s width at the toes and secure heel; try wide options if needed.
  • Arch & pronation: If you overpronate with recurring issues, trial a mild‑stability model.
  • Use case: Easy miles vs tempo vs race day — one shoe rarely excels at everything.

Common mistakes

  • Buying too small — a cause of toenail trauma and blisters.
  • Assuming a carbon plate will fix form problems.
  • Ignoring outsole durability for heavy pavement mileage.
