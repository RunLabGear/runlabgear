Buying checklist

  • Choose a daily trainer you’ll be comfortable in 80% of the time.
  • Check width options if you have narrow or wide feet.
  • Rotate with a speed shoe for harder workouts.

FAQs

  • Are neutral shoes right for me? If you don’t overpronate heavily, they probably are.
  • Do I need multiple neutral shoes? One is enough, but rotation improves durability and reduces injury risk.
  • Are cushioned shoes slower? Heavier, yes — but many neutral shoes now balance comfort with responsiveness.
