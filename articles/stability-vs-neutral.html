Neutral vs Stability Running Shoes — Which to Choose?

Support tech has evolved. Modern “stability” shoes guide your stride without harsh posts. Here’s how to decide.

Quick Decision

  • Neutral: If you don’t have a history of overuse injuries and your wear pattern is even.
  • Stability: If you overpronate visibly or get recurring shin, knee or arch issues.

Try‑On Checklist

  • Thumb’s width in toe box, no heel slip.
  • Mild inward guidance is fine; avoid shoes that force your foot out.
  • Comfort wins: if it feels wrong in-store, it won’t “break in.”
